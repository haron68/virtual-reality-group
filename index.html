History of VR

December 12th, 2018 by Katherine Xu

History of VR

The idea of virtual reality was thought to have arisen in 1935 when Stanley Weinbaum released a fictional story called “Pygmalion’s Spectacles”. Developments for the actual technology didn’t begin until around the 1950s.

  • 1956

    The first VR machine was created by Morton Heilig, called the Snesorama. This large machine was meant to stimulate all of the senses and utilized 3D video, audio, movements, smell and atmosphere effects to mimic a simulation.

  • 1960

    First head-mounted display (HMD) was patented by Helig.

  • 1961

    First HMD with motion tracking was created b Comeau and Bryan.

  • 1965

    The concept of the “Ultimate Display” was envisioned. This concept incorporated computer hardware to enable users to interact with objects.

  • 1975

    The first interactive VR platform, VIDEOPLACE, was developed by Myron Krueger, a computer artist. This platform used computer graphics, projectors, video cameras, displays, and motion-detecting technology.

  • 1985

    VPL Research was founded by Jaron Lanier and Thomas Zimmerman. This was the first company to sell VR goggles, including DataGlove, EyePhone HMD, and Audio Sphere.

  • 1991

    The first VR system to drive robots on Mars was designed by Antonio Medina, who was a scientist at NASA. Virtuality Group launched Virtuality, which was a company that created VR arcade machines that used headsets.

  • 2007

    Google created Street View, which allowed users on Google Maps see surroundings on certain locations that was full 360 view and interactive.

  • 2010

    Palmer Luckey, an 18 year old entrepreneur, created the first prototype of Oculus Rift. This was a headset that had a 90 degree field of vision and HMD. This headset utilized a computer’s processing power to help bring 3D images to the user.

  • 2012

    Kickstarter for Oculus Rift was started and $2.4 million was raised.

  • 2014

    Oculus Rift was bought by Facebook for $2 billion. This was a big step for the field of VR and interest in VR grew rapidly after this. Google launched Cardboard, which was a cheap VR knockoff that smartphone users could use. Samsung announced their Samsung Gear VR, which was a headset that utilized Samsung smartphones.

  • 2016

    Hundreds of companies began deveoping VR products, such as Apple, Samsung, Amazon, Microsoft, HTC, etc.
