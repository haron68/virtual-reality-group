Creativity and Entertainment Aspects on Virtual Reality

OVERVIEW

One of the main reasons for virtual reality being a popular cybertechnology is the rapid development of interactive gaming experience within the last few years. Currently, Google, Sony, Oculus (acquired by Facebook in 2014) and HTC are leading the virtual reality products market, and promote and release their own type of virtual reality headsets. Unity and Unreal Engine are the major and common game development engines for any level of creators, and the users to build their own application on different platforms since the engine provide different plug-ins and integrate different software development kit for cell phone operating systems, such as google SDK is available and supported for Apple iOS and Android at the same time. In the last few years, Virtual Reality technology is getting more attention with affordable price on their equipment, and more virtual reality games have been released than ever since we have created virtual reality as a cybertechnology.

ENTERTAINMENT

Types of Virtual Reality devices for single player at home They are available to purchase on the market for personal use. We can divide Virtual Reality headsets/displays into two major types, the first one is portable, such as Google Cardboard, Samsung Gear VR, Oculus Go, etc. And, the second one is non-portable, they are connected to a console or computer for room-scale virtual reality, such as Oculus Rift, HTC Vive, Playstation VR headset, etc. As a personal entertainment gaming device Traditional gaming console company Sony Playstation and smartphone tech company HTC both released their VR headset in 2016. Since then, different gaming developing companies are designed more games or add VR mode into their games for VR headset experience, such as Biohazard 7 and Skyrim in 2017. In the early development of VR gaming experience, they usually integrate mission/task-based or first-person shooting story into their games and tried to gain more users to buy their headsets. As a Special Social Entertainment with commercial level virtual reality experience Zero Latency, Australia tech company, one of the VR experience companies to start this new industry with warehouse scale, free-roam, multiplayer virtual reality entertainment. In this website, “Free-roam virtual reality (FRVR) let’s you and your friends explore virtual worlds like you do in real life. It’s next level social entertainment. You’re taken inside a game, where your body is the controller and your mind believes it’s real.” This company also built a special project for the world's first virtual reality marriage proposal in 2017. From the article in VRscout, “It was then that Kelly was transported to a painstakingly recreated version of her favorite place in the world, her grandparent’s home in South Africa. The virtual environment was based on images pulled from Google Earth and Street View, where an artist recreated the building and trees in the area…” The company built the project based on what Alex shows to them, and the information they gathered from Google. They are able to recreate Kelly’s favorite place in the world. Alex invites Kelly to Zero Latency Zombie VR experience, and it turns out to be a successful surprise marriage proposal.

CREATIVITY

As a new form of Art Often people will think about first-person shooting games, or task/mission based games when they hear about Virtual Reality experience. However, in the blog article “The most relaxing VR games” by Samuel Horti form PC Gamer, they say “Stepping into a VR space is often more intense than playing a regular game with a mouse and keyboard: blasting enemies in a VR shooter means twisting and turning on the spot to find your foes, while VR horror games are guaranteed to give you nightmares. But being fully present in another world is also a chance to banish your real-life worries for a few hours.” At the same article, they introduce 14 relaxing VR games for the users, which are including Chroma Lab, Tilt Brush, Guided Meditation VR, Nature Treks VR, etc. Recently, the department of art at the University of Minnesota is promoting Virtual Reality as a new medium of art, and avocadate artists to try out their headsets for making more creative art pieces via the virtual world. In their open Virtual Reality studio, they provides two stations for the students. One station contains a HTC Vive headset connected to high-performance computer, a pair of controllers for both hands, a projector for showing the live visual in the headset, a base station for creating room-scale virtual reality and wireless tracking the headset and controllers location in the virtual reality space. In the VR experience at the studio, they will set up everything and open “Tilt Brush” a VR software created by Google. In the software description, “Tilt Brush lets you paint in 3D space with virtual reality. Unleash your creativity with three-dimensional brush strokes, stars, light, and even fire. Your room is your canvas. Your palette is your imagination. The possibilities are endless.” So, the software is intended for people to spend time with creative ideas to build different objects in the VR space.

TOOL FOR DEMOING ARTWORKS

There are some websites provide online virtual reality services for demonstrate 3D models, and photographs, such as Sketchfab, InstaVR, etc. For example, Sketchfab is 3D model trading platform, users can buy and sell their 3D models in their site. They also provide an instant virtual reality service. You can even import photos from your social media account, and your personal VR gallery will be ready in just a few minutes. In my personal experience, I made a Virtual Reality gallery for my photography class final project to show my photos. I used the development software Unity to build a VR app via Xcode on my laptop to my cell phone. In the app, I integrated C# script to control user movement, 3D models from Sketchfab, and my original photographs together to make my personal VR gallery. Also, photography based social network Flickr provides their platform for sharing VR photos and let other users to enjoy VR experience photos on their site.
