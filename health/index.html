Impacts of Virtual Reality on Behavior

While the technology for widespread and applicable virtual reality is not currently in sight, it is still in the works and could potentially be a large part of everyone’s lives. Virtual reality is an open canvas that can be used for many things, such as entertainment, communication and even healthcare. Because there are limitless possibilities for virtual reality, what people use it for could potentially affect how they act outside of a virtual world. Things people experience shape who they are and if virtual reality can provide those experiences then there is great potential that this new technology could become something that drives change in how people act. These behavioral effects of virtual reality is already a subject being explored and because the technology is advancing it is important to learn how it could affect the lives of those who use it. Whether the technology could be used to promote better behavior or the use of virtual reality could increase violent impulses is a subject that needs to be examined in order to gauge how virtual reality will affect people.

As virtual reality continues to develop, there is a concern that experiencing or acting out violent actions in virtual reality will cause the person to become more aggressive in the real world. There is an argument that people will behave more aggressively due to a more realistic and immersive experience, and there are others who believe that the release of violent tendencies in a virtual world would help make the general population more peaceful. This parallels an issue that is still being explored today with violent video games. There are many studies today that explore the effects of violent video games on human behavior and these studies could become very important when examining how violent encounters, experienced with virtual reality, could potentially impact the behavior of regular people since the experiences are much more immersive that today’s video games.

Virtual reality may also prove to be useful in inhibiting social communication. When having a conversation using virtual reality, the real life pressures are taken away from communicating with someone that the person is not familiar with. Facebook commissioned a study to explore how people responded to having conversations in virtual reality, in order to get a better view on how people respond to the technology. The study was simply having two participants, who did not know each other, have a conversation with virtual reality through virtual avatars. Participants wore EEG headsets in order to monitor their brain waves during their conversations. They found that people generally responded positively to having their conversations in virtual reality. This was especially the case for introverts as they said it increased their confidence and reduced self consciousness. It was also seen that people were more comfortable in the virtual environment as people, interviewed after the conversation, said that they were surprised with how comfortable they were with discussing personal matters in the virtual environment. Using information from this study, it is seen that virtual reality could potentially be a powerful resource in helping those with deficient social skills become more confident and help develop those lacking skills. Virtual reality could be used as a safe space for people with these issues and would be the perfect, low risk place for them to practice these skills.

Virtual reality also allows people to experience what life would be like from a different person’s perspective, which can lead to more empathetic behavior. If users are able to experience what others have they are more likely to have the ability to empathise with their situation. A study was conducted in order to see virtual reality could lead to empathetic behavior. The study consisted of having the participants read information about homelessness, go through an interactive computer narrative about it, or experience the narrative with virtual reality. It was found that people who did the interactive narrative and the virtual reality experience signed a petition for helping the homeless at the same rate and both these groups signed more often than those who simply read the article. But in the weeks following the study, surveys were sent out and they found that participants using virtual reality had longer lasting positive effects of empathy than the group that did the interactive narrative. While this study shows that virtual reality does have an effect, it also shows that simply being exposed personally to the issue at hand will have a more profound effect than reading information about the issue online. Virtual reality could become a good way to have people experience all sorts of life encounters and gain a new perspective on life which could help promote better social behavior.
